The method

What a circle is, and why you stick with it

done open 30 circles = 30 days of one challenge

Every challenge runs for 30 days. Every day is a circle: empty or filled, nothing in between. Tap it in the evening, done. The calendar view shows every circle of the month at a glance. The streak just counts how many days in a row you didn't miss.

Why that keeps you going is very human: once you've done 10 days in a row, you don't want to throw that away. Losing a streak feels worse than one more day feels good, so you keep tapping. And don't worry: missing one day won't ruin everything. There's a one-day edit window and an 85% threshold for that. Only No Exceptions mode holds you to every single day.

Loss aversion: Kahneman & Tversky. Habit automaticity: Lally et al., University College London, 2010.

Questions

Before you download

How is my data handled?

Everything stays on your device. There are no servers, no accounts, and nothing is sent anywhere. No analytics, no tracking, no ads. See the full Privacy Policy for details.

What's the widget for?

A home screen widget shows your progress at a glance and lets you check off a challenge without opening the app. There's also a Control Center button for the same one-tap check-off. Both are optional.

What does 12 Challenges cost?

The core features are free forever: full tracking, iCloud sync, and the home screen widget, no subscription, no ads, no account. An optional, one-time Pro upgrade unlocks the Apple Watch app, statistics, and the full archive.

I forgot to check in yesterday. Is the challenge over now?

No. Today and yesterday can still be changed afterward, and thanks to the 85% threshold, a few missed days don't cost you the whole month. Only in No Exceptions mode does every single day really count.
